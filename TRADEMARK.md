# IterVC Trademark and Branding Policy

Modified versions of IterVC must not be distributed under the "IterVC" name
or branding. Only official builds published through the
[official repository](https://github.com/ShxwZ/IterVC) are considered verified
releases. Forks and modified versions must clearly identify themselves as
unofficial and must not imply equivalent security, reliability, compatibility,
or endorsement by the IterVC project.

## Requirements for modified versions

Modified distributions must:

- use a distinct product name and distinct branding;
- prominently display the factual attribution
  `Based on IterVC — https://github.com/ShxwZ/IterVC`;
- place that attribution in an About, Credits, or equivalent in-application
  location and in the accompanying documentation when distributing an
  executable; and
- place that attribution prominently in the README or equivalent documentation
  when distributing source code only.

Modified distributions must not:

- use the IterVC name as their product name or present IterVC branding as their
  own;
- use confusingly similar names, logos, domains, package names, application
  identifiers, or other branding;
- claim or suggest that they are official, verified, endorsed, or supported by
  the IterVC project; or
- imply that they provide security, reliability, or compatibility equivalent
  to an official IterVC release.

## Permitted references

Accurate, nominative references to IterVC are allowed when needed to describe
origin, compatibility, or relationship to the project, provided they are not
misleading and do not imply endorsement. The attribution required by the
[IterVC License](LICENSE) is one such permitted reference.

## No trademark license

Neither the IterVC License nor this policy grants a license to use the IterVC
name, logos, or branding beyond the factual references described above.
